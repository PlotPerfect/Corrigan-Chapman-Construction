Description: The works at Sobell Leisure Centre consisted of significant improvements to the reception and circulation areas, enhancing customer interactions and aiding access. A new café and seating area were created adjacent to the fitness suite and ice rink. The works were intended to enhance the entrance and exit areas of the leisure centre. The centre remained open to the public throughout the execution of the works. The project construction programme was phased to ensure uninterrupted operation, with out-of-hours working incorporated within our scope of works.

Location: Sobell Leisure Centre

Client: GLL

Duration: 21 weeks

Value: £330,000

Corrigan & Chapman Construction Limited were appointed to construct a fully functional trampoline facility within the existing indoor tennis court area. This included themed party rooms, toilets, changing and locker facilities.

The build comprised jumbo stud walling, elevated walkways, safety handrails, security systems, audio installation, decorations, floor coverings, drainage, and the installation of trampolines and accessories.

As a design and build contract, it was important to establish a strong working relationship between the Client, Architect, Structural Engineer, and Local Authority. The success of this collaboration was demonstrated by the Client's absolute satisfaction with the quality of design, the high specification achieved within budget, and the timely completion of the project.

The London Aquatics Centre project involved the internal development of a new gymnasium in the upper welcome zone. The London Aquatics Centre is a purpose-built Olympic swimming pool facility that required further internal enhancements to incorporate different activities. The works included:

  • Installation of a high-level ventilation duct above the inner entrance doors
  • Closing of existing double door sets
  • Blanking of the glass screens to add Sound Sorba and mirrors
  • Strengthening of the floor underneath the computer floor for five pieces of gym apparatus
  • Forming of two dwarf walls at either end of the upper welcome zone
  • Installation of door automation
  • Working on a live distribution board located within the buggy park cupboard
  • Modifications to the existing lighting
  • Introduction of feature lighting
  • New wiring circuits for the gym equipment
  • Extension of the perimeter heating system
  • New floor coverings
  • Minor decorations
